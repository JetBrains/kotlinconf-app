KotlinConf is committed to creating a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for everyone. We value the participation of people of all ages, genders, gender identities and expressions, sexual orientations, abilities, physical appearances, body sizes, ethnicities, nationalities, races, and religions.

Our goal is to foster a community where everyone feels safe, supported, and able to fully participate. We expect all participants to contribute to this positive atmosphere by treating others with respect and consideration, both during the conference and in all related spaces.

Any form of written or verbal communication should always be considerate and inclusive. Behaviors that undermine this environment are not acceptable. Examples include: derogatory comments related to age, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, ability, physical appearance, body size, ethnicity, nationality, race, religion, or belief; sexual language, images, behavior, materials, activities, or attire that create a sexualized environment in any conference setting; deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following; photography or recording without consent; sustained disruption of talks or activities; inappropriate physical contact; unwelcome sexual attention; repeated unwanted communication; and insults or jokes based on stereotypes.

Exhibitors, sponsors, and vendors in the expo hall or similar areas are also expected to follow this Code of Conduct. This Code of Conduct applies to all participants, including attendees, speakers, sponsors, exhibitors, staff, and volunteers, in all KotlinConf venues, event-related social gatherings, online channels, and any other spaces connected to the conference.

If you experience or witness behavior that concerns you, please contact an event staff member (identified by their official t-shirts and/or special badges) or reach out directly to Natasha Grebenshchikova at +31 651 82 11 34 or ethics@kotlinconf.com. You may also submit a report via our [online form](https://surveys.jetbrains.com/s3/kotlinconf-violations-reporting) – anonymously or with your contact details.

Participants asked to stop inappropriate behavior are expected to comply immediately. Our staff will support anyone who feels unsafe, help contacting hotel/venue security or law enforcement, provide escorts, and ensure you feel safe for the duration of the event.

We value your attendance – thank you for helping us make KotlinConf welcoming and respectful for everyone.
